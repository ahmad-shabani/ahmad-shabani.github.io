Research Agenda

Security from circuits to microarchitecture.

A research program centered on trustworthy hardware, processor security, side channels, covert channels, and practical countermeasures.

Core themes

Hardware TrustTrojan DetectionPUFsGPU SecuritySide ChannelsASIC Design

Processor and GPU security

Current postdoctoral research studies how modern processors can contain hidden vulnerabilities that leak confidential information. The direction connects microarchitectural behavior, hardware performance events, scheduling, memory systems, and security-observable side effects.

Hardware Trojan detection and Design for Hardware Trust

Doctoral research focused on efficient methodologies based on Design for Hardware Trust to detect malicious modifications inserted by untrusted foundries or third-party supply-chain actors.

Lightweight PUF-based authentication

Research contributions include lightweight protocols and implementation-aware designs where physical uniqueness and low-cost hardware primitives support secure identification and authentication.

Security across abstraction layers

The long-term vision is to reason about hardware security not only at one layer, but across the stack: physical effects, circuit structures, RTL/ASIC design, microarchitecture, firmware, operating systems, and application-observable behavior.
